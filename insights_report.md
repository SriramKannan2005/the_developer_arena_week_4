# 📋 E-Commerce Sales Analysis — Insights Report
**Period:** January 2024 – April 2024  
**Dataset:** 100 transactions | 5 products | 4 regions  
**Total Revenue:** ₹12,364,048

---

## Executive Summary

This report analyses 100 sales transactions from a fictional e-commerce store selling
consumer electronics across four Indian regions. The dataset spans the first quarter
of 2024 plus early April, covering products from Headphones to Laptops.

---

## 📈 Insight 1 — Revenue Trend: Strong Q1 With a February Dip

| Month     | Revenue    | MoM Change |
|-----------|------------|------------|
| Jan 2024  | ₹4,120,524 | —          |
| Feb 2024  | ₹2,656,050 | −35.5%     |
| Mar 2024  | ₹4,485,006 | +68.9%     |
| Apr 2024* | ₹1,103,468 | partial    |

**Finding:** February saw a sharp revenue dip of 35.5%. March recovered strongly to become the
best month on record — likely driven by fiscal year-end spending and promotional events.
The February trough warrants investigation; possible causes include fewer transactions,
lower-value products selling, or a genuine seasonal slowdown.

**Recommendation:** Plan targeted mid-quarter promotions (e.g., Valentine's Day bundles)
to prevent the February dip in future years.

## 🏆 Insight 2 — Product Performance: Laptops Rule

| Product    | Total Revenue | Share  | Avg Unit Price |
|------------|--------------|--------|----------------|
| Laptop     | ₹3,889,210   | 31.5%  | ₹30,600        |
| Tablet     | ₹2,884,340   | 23.3%  | ₹26,700        |
| Phone      | ₹2,859,394   | 23.1%  | ₹22,100        |
| Headphones | ₹1,384,033   | 11.2%  | ₹22,800        |
| Monitor    | ₹1,348,071   | 10.9%  | ₹23,900        |

**Finding:** Laptops generate 31.5% of total revenue despite a higher price point,
indicating strong demand. Tablets and Phones are nearly tied at ~23% each.
Headphones and Monitors are the weakest performers, together accounting for only 22%.

**Recommendation:** Prioritize Laptop inventory and create bundled offers
(e.g., Laptop + Headphones) to lift the tail products.

## 🗺️ Insight 3 — Regional Performance: North & South Lead

| Region | Revenue    | Share  |
|--------|------------|--------|
| North  | ₹3,983,635 | 32.2%  |
| South  | ₹3,737,852 | 30.2%  |
| East   | ₹2,519,639 | 20.4%  |
| West   | ₹2,123,922 | 17.2%  |

**Finding:** North and South collectively contribute 62.4% of total revenue. East and West
significantly underperform. This may reflect differences in population density, logistics
infrastructure, or marketing reach.

**Recommendation:** Invest in East and West logistics and regional marketing partnerships
to close the 40% revenue gap with the top two regions.

---

## 🔬 Insight 4 — Price vs Demand Sweet Spot

The scatter plot of Price vs Quantity reveals a clear cluster of high-revenue transactions
in the ₹15,000–₹40,000 unit price range with 3–8 units per order.

- Transactions **below ₹10,000** (Headphones) tend to have higher quantities but lower revenue.
- Transactions **above ₹40,000** (premium Laptops/Monitors) have high revenue per order
  but lower quantities — they are less frequent.

**Recommendation:** The ₹20,000–₹35,000 range maximises both volume and margin.
Focus new product introductions and promotions in this price band.

---

## 🌐 Insight 5 — Regional Product Skews (Heatmap Analysis)

The Product × Region heatmap shows notable skews:

- **Laptops:** Consistently strong across all regions
- **Tablets:** Strong in North (₹0.9M+) but relatively weak in East
- **Monitors:** Unexpectedly strong in South
- **Phones:** Relatively even distribution — good flagship product

**Recommendation:** Run region-specific campaigns: Tablet promotions in East/West,
Monitor deals in North, Laptop bundles everywhere.

---

## ✅ Final Recommendations

1. **February recovery campaign** — seasonal deals to prevent mid-quarter dip
2. **Laptop inventory + bundling** — capitalise on the top-performing category
3. **East & West expansion** — targeted logistics and regional marketing
4. **Price-band focus** — keep new product launches in the ₹20K–₹35K sweet spot
5. **Regional product targeting** — Tablets in East, Monitors in North
